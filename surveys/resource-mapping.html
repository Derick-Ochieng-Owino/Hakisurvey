What is Resource Mapping?

Resource mapping utilizes remote sensing, GIS (Geographic Information Systems), and field verification to accurately locate, quantify, and monitor natural resources such as minerals, timber, water, and agricultural land cover. This data is critical for sustainable management, accurate financial valuation, and regulatory compliance.

We provide decision-makers with detailed, up-to-date geospatial intelligence necessary to optimize extraction, protect environmental assets, and plan future land use.

Volume Quantification

Accurately calculating stockpiles, mine pit volumes, or timber yield estimates.

Environmental Monitoring

Tracking changes in water bodies, erosion, and forest health over time.

Extraction Planning

Designing efficient, safe, and environmentally compliant mining or forestry operations.

Asset Inventory

Creating high-detail inventories of fixed and mobile assets within the resource area.

The Resource Mapping Process: Integration of Data

Our methodology integrates aerial data capture with powerful GIS analysis to create actionable intelligence.

1. Remote Sensing Data Acquisition

We acquire high-resolution imagery and LiDAR data depending on the resource type and site size.

  • UAV/Drone Imagery: Collecting ultra-high resolution imagery for small to medium sites (mines, stockpiles).
  • LiDAR and Satellite: Utilizing LiDAR (Light Detection and Ranging) for elevation, and multi-spectral satellite imagery for large-scale land cover and vegetation analysis.

High-resolution aerial data collection provides the foundation for volume and cover analysis.

2. GIS and Geospatial Analysis

The raw data is processed and layered within a GIS environment for detailed spatial analysis.

  • Feature Extraction: Automatically classifying land cover (e.g., distinguishing healthy trees from sparse vegetation or water bodies).
  • Geostatistical Modeling: Using techniques like kriging to estimate the distribution and quantity of resources across the site.

A GIS map overlay used to visualize and prioritize resource exploration or extraction zones.

3. Volumetric and Inventory Reporting

We generate precise quantitative reports, essential for accounting, inventory, and legal compliance.

  • Stockpile Calculation: Calculating the volume of material stockpiles (sand, aggregate, coal) using DTM differencing for audit purposes.
  • Forest Inventory: Estimating tree height, density, and biomass across forestry blocks for yield prediction.

Precise volumetric measurement for financial auditing and operational planning.
