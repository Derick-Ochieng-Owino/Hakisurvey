What is Boundary Demarcation?

Boundary demarcation is the physical act of marking a legally defined property line on the ground. It is the practical component that follows a cadastral survey, involving the placement of permanent, durable markers (beacons, pegs, or monuments) at the corner points of a parcel of land.

This service is essential when boundary markers have been lost, damaged, or were never installed, providing clear, physical definition for construction, fencing, landscaping, and confirming legal setbacks.

The Demarcation Process: From Plan to Peg

Our licensed surveyors follow documented legal procedures to convert digital coordinates into tangible markers on your land.

1. Legal Plan Interpretation

The surveyor first reviews the cadastral plan, deed description, and corner records to mathematically compute the exact geographic coordinates of the boundary corners.

  • Title Data Review: Validating coordinates, bearings, and distances from the official government records.
  • Adjoining Records: Analyzing neighboring property data to ensure the new demarcation aligns with the established legal framework.

Initial office work involves interpreting legal records to calculate exact corner coordinates.

2. Field Survey and Monument Location

The calculated coordinates are translated to the field using high-precision GNSS/Total Station equipment.

  • Establish Control: Setting up reliable geodetic control points near the site.
  • Stakeout: Precisely locating the calculated corner points using the control network.
  • Monument Search: Searching for evidence of existing monuments to verify or correct the calculated position.

Using RTK GNSS to guide the placement of the physical marker to the calculated point.

3. Monument Placement and Certification

The final step is installing the durable markers and formally documenting their position.

  • Permanent Markers: Installing official boundary monuments (e.g., concrete beacons, iron rods, or durable pegs) at the verified corner locations.
  • Witnessing: Recording supplementary measurements to nearby permanent features to ensure the marker can be accurately relocated if disturbed.
  • Demarcation Plan: Issuing a formal certificate or plan showing the final position of all placed markers.

The physical placement of a durable, legally recognized boundary beacon.

When Do You Need Boundary Demarcation?

Lost or Damaged Markers

When original boundary pegs have been removed, destroyed, or paved over, requiring professional re-establishment.

Before Major Construction

Mandatory prior to any major excavation or foundation work near the property line to prevent encroachment.

Boundary Fencing Projects

Ensuring a new fence, wall, or hedge is built exactly on the legal line to avoid disputes with neighbors.

Dispute Prevention

Proactively marking boundaries before a disagreement arises, providing documented clarity for all parties.
